FAQ

Application Procedures
1.Who can apply for this international summer session program?

SIE Summer Sessions attracts a wide range of people from different backgrounds. Any student who is currently enrolled in a full-time and accredited college or university is eligible to apply. High school seniors with demonstrated English proficiency and academic excellence are also welcome to apply for our program. People who are neither undergraduates nor high school seniors may also apply for the program and SIE Admissions Committee will evaluate their candidacy on an individual basis.

2. When can I apply for this program?

The Application for 2017 SIE Summer Sessions has now started, and an up to $650 tuition discount is provided for students who pay their fees before December 31, 2016. For further information, please refer to Academic Calendar.

3. How can I apply for this program?

BJTU/ECNU undergraduates can apply at Academic Affairs Office. The credits earned will be recognized as elective course credits. The office will release the application notification in every May, please follow the update of Academic Affairs Office website.

All non-BJTU/ECNU must complete the application online. For the detailed application procedure, please refer to How to Apply.

4. When will I receive the Welcoming Letter after completing the application?

It usually takes about three business days for the welcoming email to arrive. You could continue the payment or choosing class after receiving the letter.

5. How can I confirm that I have already been enrolled in courses?

When you finish choosing courses, you will be informed by the system that you are “successfully enrolled”. You can check your enrollment information by logging into your SIE system. Should there be any questions, please contact us.

6. Who should I contact when I have problems with my online application?

BJTU Summer Sessions students can contact us by Email: iss@sieschool.org or call (0086) 10-51692938, while ECNU Summer Sessions students can reach us by Email: iss@ied.ecnu.edu.cn or call (0086) 021-32514240. We will be more than happy to help.

7.When are the application deadlines?

Early application deadline December 31, 2016; regular application just need before the summer session start. For detail information, please refer to Academic Calendar.

8. What type(s) of visa do I need to study at the summer program?

If you are not a Chinese citizen, you need to apply for the Chinese visa that allows you to stay in China for at least the length of the program. Please note that for short study programs, you shall apply for Business Visa (F) instead of Student Visa (X).The Chinese Embassy does not accept mail-in visa applications. You may go to the closest Chinese Embassy and submit appropriate forms and relevant documents. If you cannot apply in person, you may apply through a Chinese Visa Service Agency for a small fee. To find an agency, simply search “Chinese Visa Service” online.

Here are some recommended agencies:

http://www.mychinavisa.com/china-visa/business-visa/

http://www.flychina.com/chinavisa.asp

For more information concerning the visa application process, please go to the official website of the Chinese Embassy: http://www.china-embassy.org/eng/

9. What kind of academic experience will you get from SIE Summer School?

SIE Summer School strives to introduce the essence of American Education to China, with all courses teaching by experienced professors from renowned American colleges and universities, such as Harvard University, Yale University, UC-Berkeley, UCLA, UVa, Middlebury College, Claremont McKenna College, etc., including some leading authorities in the academic fields and business elites. Please check here for detailed information of the Faculties.

10. (For non-native English speakers) What are the language requirements for the program?

Non-native, English-speaking students must demonstrate that he/she is proficient in English and can comprehend the courses. SIE Summer School accepts any one of the following English language proficiency certificate:

1.Studying or graduated from an institution where English is the main language of instruction

2. With a GPA above 2.5

3. Have a valid TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) score higher than 80 (Internet-based Test) or equivalent scores in other forms of TOEFL tests

4. Have a valid IELTS (International English Language Testing System) score which is higher than 6.0

5. For Chinese undergraduates, successfully passed CET-4

6. Other equivalent standard tests

If none of requirement the above is satisfied, students may also request a phone interview conducted in English by the SIE recruiting officers (Admission officer) in lieu of providing examination scores. Successful completion of the phone interviews will be sufficient for matriculation into the SIE summer programs. Those students must contact SIE at iss@sieschool.org (Beijing) / iss@ied.ecnu.edu.cn (Shanghai) to request a phone interview, or their application will not be considered complete.Contact Us

Courses
1. Do I need to buy my own textbooks?

Textbooks are not provided and students are required to secure their textbooks before the classes start, or their class performance will be greatly affected. In 2017, e-books for all courses are available on Amazon, which is convenient and environmentally-friendly. Students are strongly recommended to purchase the required textbooks for each course thereafter. For further information, please refer to Course List.

2.Can I withdraw from the courses?

Yes. You can drop a class in the online registration system until the second day after summer session starts. After that, you can withdraw from the course with a signed withdrawal form from the professor in charge of the course. Please note that there will be no refund for withdrawal after the second day of the Summer Session. For further information, please refer to Refund Policies .

3. Can I audit any other courses that I am not enrolled in?

No. Any form of auditing is not allowed in International Summer Sessions. For more information, please contact us.

4. Is there any chance for free trial?

We would allow limited number of students to try our course for free, before the summer sessions start. Please keep following our updates on the official website and WeChat.

5.Can I change my courses?

Yes, you can change your course registration until the second day of the Summer Session. However, since each course has limited spots, course changes may be unavailable for popular ones. We strongly suggest you do a thorough research of all the courses before registration.

6. What is the average class size?

There will be 20 students at most in English Writing, Spanish and Public Speaking classes. In other classes, the average class size is around 30 students, with a limit of 50. The actual number may vary but not deviate much from this.

7. Where can I find course information, especially prerequisites for each course?

Information concerning course levels and prerequisites can be found in the. Course List

8. Can I get college credits from the courses I take at the summer program?

All courses offered by our programs meet the standards of their equivalents taught in the United States. In the past 2 years, over 2500 students have successfully transferred their credits back to their home institutions, including University of California at Berkeley, Purdue University, Boston University, etc. Please note that different colleges and universities may differ regarding credit-transfer policies. We strongly recommend you to consult with the registrar’s office of your home institution before applying for our programs.

Should you need any assistance or any supplementary materials, BJTU Summer Sessions students can contact us by Email: iss@sieschool.org or call (0086) 10-51692938, while ECNU Summer Sessions students can reach us by Email: iss@ied.ecnu.edu.cn or call (0086) 021-32514240.

Exams
1. Where should I request the transcript to be sent to?

Many U.S. universities and colleges only accept the official transcripts that are sent to the school directly. Hence, we recommend students to state the address of the relevant administrative office of your home school in the "Address which the transcript will be sent to" in your online application.

2. How do I make transcript requests?

Students will receive the transcripts around 4-6 weeks after the program ends (please don’t forget to provide your transcript address in the application system correctly and completely). If you request extra copies of the transcript, you must complete the Transcript Request Form. You can get the Transcript Request Form in the Administrative Office of each campus, or request one online by email: bjtuiss@bjtu.edu.cn (Beijing) / iss@ied.ecnu.edu.cn (Shanghai). Each additional copy of the official transcript costs 50 RMB (About $7.3), postage not included.

3.When is the final exam period?

The final exams will take place on the last two days of each summer session program, subjected to professors’ arrangements.

Campus Facilities
1. What campus facilities are available to me?

The track field, outdoor basketball court and study rooms are all free of charge. The tennis court, badminton court, table tennis court, indoor basketball court and swimming pool are available with extra cost. In addition, students will receive a campus card upon registration, which could be used in school canteen with average cost lower to 1 dollar.

2.Is it convenient to dine around campus?

There are many places to eat around campus apart from school canteen. We will provide a list of nearby restaurants and take-out food upon registration.

Cities
1. What about off-campus activities?

Beijing and Shanghai are two major cities in China, which combine modernity and traditional culture. A huge variety of leisure activities ranging from shopping to sightseeing are available for you. SIE Social Events provides you opportunities for networking with thousands of students and eminent professors.

2. Is airport pick-up provided?

There will be airport pick-up services for foreign students and professors. Please send your pick-up service requests to bjtuiss@bjtu.edu.cn (Beijing) / iss@ied.ecnu.edu.cn (Shanghai). Please note that students who request the pick-up service need to pay for their own transportation cost from the airport to the campus.

3. What is the average cost of living in each city?

Students are recommended to budget around 50 RMB (about $7.3) per day. However, the daily cost varies hugely for individuals due to their different consumption levels.
