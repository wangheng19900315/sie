Academic Standards

The academic standards of ECNU/BJTU ISS stand strictly in line with ECNU's/BJTU's policies and are supervised by ECNU/BJTU Teaching Affairs Office and the International Students Office. Besides, ECNU/BJTU ISS has its own Academic Committee formed by several top-notch professors from prestigious North American universities to supervise the academic quality of the program. Director of Academic Committee has the responsibility to keep in touch with ECNU/BJTU to follow ECNU's/BJTU's advices.

Courses

ECNU/BJTU ISS allows students to enroll at most two courses for the 4-week program and two or three courses for ECNU 5-week program. Auditing is not allowed in any class.

Each class has a maximum number of enrolled students (20 for English Composition ,Public Speaking and Spanish, 35 for Accounting, 50 for the rest courses). When the course is fully enrolled, the application of the course will be closed and students can only be added to waiting list.

The attendance follows the related regulations of ECNU/BJTU. For details, please refer to the Student Handbook upon orientation.

Course Format

All courses of ECNU/BJTU ISS are instructed by qualified professors from universities in North-America and follow the same structure and standard of most U.S. universities.

1) All courses are instructed in English and offered as lectures.

2) In the 4 week programs, our courses meet for 20 sessions @ 135 minutes for 2700 minutes of classroom instruction. In the 5 week program, our courses meet for 25 sessions @ 110 minutes for 2750 minutes of classroom instruction. In addition, faculty are available in their offices for additional instruction for 135 minutes every week in the 4 week program and 100 minutes in the 5 week program. All courses strictly follow US university course format to ensure that students can easily transfer credits back to their home institutions.

3) Each course will be awarded 4 credits from the BJTU/ECNU. Students are advised to consult with their university registrars in advance to see how credit for transfer courses is awarded.

4) Each class has a maximum of 50 enrolled students (except for English Composition, Public Speaking, Spanish and Accounting which have lower limits).

Add/Drop/Switch Course

Students may add, drop or switch course before prescribed deadline (maximum 2 courses for 4-week session for 5-week session). Students who want to add or switch courses need to make sure the courses applied for is still available.
Add course: Add the course in online application system after payment before the summer session starts (maximum 2 courses for 4-week session and 3 courses for 5-week session). After summer session starts please download the Add Form, fill out the form then return it back to the Administration Office.
 
Withdraw Course: Download the Withdraw Form, fill out the form and return it back to the Administration Office and confirm about the refund.
 
Drop Course Deadline
Students can drop course before prescribed deadline. Please download the Drop Form (BJTU-4; ECNU-4; ECNU-5), fill out the form with the instructor’s signature as agreement of dropping course and return to the Administration Office. The course dropped won’t show on the transcript.

Please note that dropping course won’t involve any refund, for more information please read the Refund Policy.


Credits

ECNU/BJTU ISS awards credits based on students' final grades, and students must pass their courses to receive credit for them. Each course is awarded 4 credits on transcripts.For the detailed course grading systems and policies, please refer to the following ECNU/BJTU grading systems and policies.

BJTU grading system; ECNU grading system

During the application process, each student is strongly encouraged to work with his/her home institution’s Study Abroad Office or Registrar’s Office or Academic Advisor to determine what courses of the International Summer Sessions are accepted for credit transfer and to clarify the credit transfer procedures.

Academic Integrity

All students are expected to present their own work in their homework, assignments, exams and all other forms of assessment. Cheating in any form will not be tolerated and can result in expulsion from the International Summer Sessions.

The ECNU/BJTU ISS reserve the right to expel any student who purposely damages school property, threatens the safety of any member of the university community, disrupts the educational process and/or orderly operation of the university/ International Summer Sessions, or violates any local law. All students need to sign the Academic Integrity Policy at the student orientation.
