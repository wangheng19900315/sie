Research Program: Learning How Scientists Study the World Around Them (not for college credit)

Tentative Course Date and Time: Saturdays 9-11:30am (July 8th to July 29th)
Place: Classroom to be determined (will be held at BJTU)
Course Cost: $1500 USD for non-SIE students; If you are a SIE student, then your discounted cost is $1350 USD!
Course Enrollment Cap: 18 students (sign up now!)
Minimum Program Requirement: Senior High School Student
Required Textbook: There is no textbook used for this program.
Required Materials: Personal computer and software (e.g., word processing, statistics)
Program Summary: A fun and amazing 4-week experiential program led by a leading US scientist who will expose participants to the various techniques used by many global social scientists to study the world around us. Participants will work in groups to develop novel research projects that will be presented at the end of the program. In addition to getting hands-on exposure/experience to the research process, participants of the program will also learn how to apply to prestigious international science organizations (e.g., Sigma Xi), and to develop more effective resumes for future jobs and/or college/graduate school applications anywhere in the world. This is a great and rare opportunity to get exposed to the research process from an established researcher and to distinguish yourself from other students. And, because it is not for credit, participants of the program do not have to worry about failing this program! Gaining such experiences can help you eventually join prestigious scientific organizations, present research at global scientific conferences, and even develop a scientific research study!
 
Last year, a group of students participated in this program during summer of 2016. We not only had a lot of fun, but we also got a lot of things done! By the end of the program, the students were able to become members of Sigma Xi, have several research posters get accepted at scientific conferences in the US, and have a research manuscript submitted to a major international research journal. They also developed a professional resume/vita! Wow! And, you can too!
 
For more information about the program and to learn why this would be a great course to take this summer, then please contact Dr. Chang at changec@umich.edu.
